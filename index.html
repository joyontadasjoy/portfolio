The heart circulates oxygen and nutrient-rich blood throughout the body. When it's not working properly, just about everything is affected. The pulse rate is a measurement of the heart rate or the number of times the heart beats per minute. As the heart pushes blood through the arteries, the arteries expand and contract with the flow of the blood. Taking a pulse not only measures the heart rate but also can indicate the following: Heart rhythm, Strength of the pulse. Integrated System for Monitoring Pulse Rate Using Finger Tip
Abstract

This work involves the design and demonstration of a pulse rate monitoring system in real time. The goal of this project is to construct an integrated system that monitors the pulse rate through a finger via IR and provides the pulse rate in beat per minute (BPM) and displays it through LCD. We used a pulse sensor when a finger is placed on it, the reflected light changes according to the amount of blood in the capillaries. This change in transmission and reflection of light can be obtained as a pulse from the output of the pulse sensor.

Objective

Our primary goal is to provide simple, portable and alternative solution for ECG monitoring and heart rate for the people of low-middle-income groups.

IoT Based Liquefied Petroleum Gas Leakage Detection System.
Abstract

To design and implement IoT based LPG gas leakage detector. The detection system includes an alarm an LCD panel to alert the users.

Description

Gas detection and leakage are big issues in our daily lives nowadays. LPG gas is extremely combustible and dangerous to both people and property. The development of trustworthy methods for detecting gas leakage has received a lot of attention in an effort to prevent such occurrences. Here, we present an LPG gas detector alarm built on the Arduino platform. This system detects gas leaks and sounds an alarm by buzzing the buzzer connected to the circuit if any are found. Utilizing the Internet of Things, our goal is to lower the hazards in the kitchen. Gas leaks in a kitchen can be found using gas sensors. The goal of this research is to identify any gas leaks in homes and to activate the standard sound and light alert.
